Key Nutrients for Pregnancy

Iron

Prevents anemia and supports oxygen transport

Sources: Red meat, spinach, lentils, fortified cereals

Calcium

Builds baby's bones and teeth

Sources: Milk, yogurt, cheese, leafy greens

Folate

Prevents neural tube defects

Sources: Leafy greens, asparagus, broccoli, legumes

Protein

Essential for baby's growth and development

Sources: Meat, fish, eggs, legumes, dairy

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common pregnancy questions

Generally, travel is safe during the second trimester (weeks 14-20). Avoid travel in the first trimester (due to miscarriage risk) and third trimester (due to delivery risk). Always get your doctor's approval before traveling. If flying, book aisle seats, stay hydrated, and get up frequently to move around.

Safe vaccines include flu shot and Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis). These can be given during any trimester. Avoid live vaccines like MMR and varicella. Always consult your healthcare provider before getting any vaccine during pregnancy.

Go to the hospital when labor begins (contractions every 5 minutes), your water breaks, you experience heavy bleeding, or have severe pain. In the third trimester, if contractions are regular and increasing in intensity, it's time to go. Also go if you have signs of emergency as listed in the warning signs section.

Sleep on your left side during pregnancy - this improves blood flow to the baby. Avoid sleeping on your back after the first trimester as it can restrict blood flow. Use pregnancy pillows for support. If you wake up on your back, simply roll to your side. Getting comfortable sleep is important for you and your baby.

Yes, morning sickness is very common and affects about 70% of pregnant women. It usually starts around week 6 and improves by week 12. To manage it, eat small frequent meals, stay hydrated, get adequate rest, and avoid triggers. Ginger tea and B6 supplements may help. Contact your doctor if you're unable to keep any food down.

Recommended weight gain depends on your pre-pregnancy BMI: Normal BMI (25-35 lbs), Overweight (15-25 lbs), Obese (11-20 lbs), Underweight (28-40 lbs). Most weight gain occurs in the second and third trimesters. Eat a balanced diet and exercise regularly. Work with your healthcare provider to ensure healthy weight gain.
